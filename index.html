01 · About

The Research

I am a PhD student in Robotics at [University Name], working at the intersection of multi-robot systems, evolutionary computation, and complex adaptive systems. My research investigates how populations of robots can self-organise, adapt, and collectively solve problems that are intractable for any single agent.

Drawing on evolutionary biology, dynamical systems theory, and machine learning, I develop frameworks for understanding emergence in robot swarms — seeking principles that hold across the boundary between the artificial and the natural.

Outside the lab I am fascinated by complexity science, synthetic biology, and the deep connections between information, life, and intelligence.

03 · Work

Projects

PRJ-001
SwarmEvo Framework
A Python framework for co-evolving robot controllers and morphologies in simulated swarm environments. Supports heterogeneous populations with configurable selection pressures and environmental challenges.
Python Evolutionary Algorithms Simulation
PRJ-002
Criticality Monitor
Real-time tooling for measuring order parameters and susceptibility in robot swarm experiments — detecting signatures of self-organised criticality across distributed sensor networks.
C++ ROS2 Complex Systems
PRJ-003
Embodied Evolution Testbed
Physical robot arena with 12 e-puck robots running on-board evolutionary algorithms. Enables study of open-ended evolution in hardware under realistic energy and communication constraints.
Hardware e-puck Embedded Systems
PRJ-004
Neural Developmental Encoding
Investigating developmental encodings for robot neural architectures, inspired by biological gene regulatory networks. Enables compact genotype representations that generalise across morphologies.
Neural Networks Developmental Biology Neuroevolution
PRJ-005
Open-Ended Ecology Simulator
A multi-agent simulation platform for studying ecological dynamics in artificial environments — predator-prey coevolution, niche construction, and the emergence of cooperation.
Artificial Life Agent-Based Modelling Julia
PRJ-006
This Website
A personal portfolio built with vanilla HTML/CSS/JS. The background simulates a stochastic spreading process on a hexagonal lattice — a nod to criticality research and the beauty of complex systems.
HTML/CSS Canvas API Criticality
